Research interests: cosmology, gravitational lensing, Cosmic Microwave Background, statistical methods

My research is driven by some of the big, unanswered questions in cosmology. Are tensions between the results of several recent cosmological surveys indicating a problem with the standard cosmological model, or are they caused by systematic errors? Is our understanding of structure formation correct, and can we accurately model the structures of the most massive dark matter halos in the Universe? What are the processes that drive astrophysical feedback? Very broadly, I am interested in developing and implementing new methods to answer these questions using galaxy surveys, cosmic microwave background surveys, and cross-correlations between the two. I especially enjoy developing novel approaches to extract science from data; in general, my work tends to lie at the intersection of data analysis and theory.

I am currently a Kavli Senior Fellow in the Kavli Institute for Cosmology at the University of Cambridge. Before arriving at Cambridge, I was a postdoc at the University of Pennsylvania. And before that, I did a PhD at the University of Chicago under the supervision of Prof. Scott Dodelson. In January, I'll be moving to the Institute for Astronomy at the University of Hawaii.
